🇨🇿 Czech Tax Guide - RSU & ESPP

How to properly report stock compensation income in your Czech tax return.

Czech Tax Rates (2025)

The Czech Republic uses a progressive tax system:

Income Level Tax Rate
Up to 1 676 052 CZK 15%
Above 1 676 052 CZK 23%

This applies to your total taxable income including employment income, RSU/ESPP income, and capital gains.

Tax Exemptions for Share Sales

  • CZK 100,000 threshold: If total annual gross income from ALL share sales < CZK 100,000 → fully exempt
  • 3-year holding rule: If shares held > 3 years AND total gross proceeds < CZK 40,000,000 → exempt
  • Losses from exempt shares cannot be offset against taxable gains

Acquisition Price (Cost Basis)

Plan Type Acquisition Price Explanation
RSU FMV at vest date Fair Market Value when shares vested
ESPP FMV at purchase date FMV (not discounted price!) because discount was already taxed as income

Exchange Rate Rules

⚠️ Important: You can choose between daily CNB rates OR yearly average (GFŘ) rates, but must be consistent for all transactions in the tax year.

For Capital Gains (Yearly Average Method)

Component Exchange Rate to Use
Sale Proceeds Yearly average rate for SALE year
Cost Basis (Acquisition Price) Yearly average rate for VEST/PURCHASE year

When selling shares vested in previous years, use different yearly rates!

Capital Gains/Loss Calculation

When you sell shares, you need to calculate whether you made a profit or a loss:

Formula:
Profit/Loss = (Sale Price × Quantity × Sale Year Rate) − (Acquisition Price × Quantity × Acquisition Year Rate) − Transaction Costs

If You Made a PROFIT (Gain)

  • The profit is taxable as "other income" (unless exempt)
  • Add to your total taxable income for the year
  • Tax rate: 15% (or 23% if total income exceeds CZK 1,676,052)

If You Made a LOSS

  • Losses are not directly taxable but must still be reported
  • Losses can offset gains from other share sales in the same year
  • Losses cannot be carried forward to future years
  • Losses from exempt sales cannot offset taxable gains
💡 Tax Optimization: If you have both gains and losses in the same year, the losses reduce your taxable gains. Choose daily or yearly rates consistently to minimize your tax.

Example: RSU Capital Gains Calculation

You sell 100 shares that vested in 2025:

Sale price per share: $40
FMV at vest (acquisition price): $30
Exchange rate (2025): 21.84 CZK/USD

Income: $40 × 100 × 21.84 = CZK 87,360
Expenses: $30 × 100 × 21.84 = CZK 65,520
Profit: 87,360 − 65,520 = CZK 21,840 (taxable)

Example: ESPP Capital Gains Calculation

You sell 100 ESPP shares purchased in 2025:

Sale price per share: $35
FMV at purchase: $30 (this is the acquisition price!)
Discounted purchase price: $25 (already taxed as income)
Exchange rate (2025): 21.84 CZK/USD

Income: $35 × 100 × 21.84 = CZK 76,440
Expenses: $30 × 100 × 21.84 = CZK 65,520
Profit: 76,440 − 65,520 = CZK 10,920 (taxable)
Note: The FMV at purchase ($30) is used as acquisition price, NOT the discounted price ($25), because the discount benefit was already declared as employment income.

Tax Return Reporting

  • Gross income from share sales and related expenses go on Line 40 of Czech PITR
  • Also report in Attachment 2 (Lines 207 and 208)
  • Losses must be declared but can only offset gains from other securities in the same year
  • Losses cannot be carried forward to future years
